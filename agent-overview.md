# Agent Overview

## Background

Agentic AI is becoming an important part of workflow automation, decision support, and operational design. In practice, useful agents do more than generate text. They help users complete work, navigate decisions, route information, summarize context, and support repeatable processes.

Strong agent design depends on selecting the right use cases, defining workflow boundaries clearly, and building the right balance between AI assistance, automation, and human review.

## Problem Statement

Many teams exploring AI agents run into common issues:

- unclear use cases
- weak workflow integration
- inconsistent outputs
- no clear escalation path
- poor evaluation discipline
- limited alignment between business value and implementation effort

Without structure, agent efforts can become interesting demos rather than useful operational tools.

## Opportunity

The opportunity is to design agentic systems that improve:

- workflow speed
- quality and consistency
- knowledge access
- routing and coordination
- documentation and summarization
- decision support in repeatable processes

## Product Framing

This portfolio example treats agentic AI as a product and operating model challenge, not just a prompt design exercise. Useful agents require:

- clear task framing
- workflow-aware design
- measurable outcomes
- human-in-the-loop decision points
- guardrails and escalation logic
- adoption planning

## Agent Design Goals

A strong agent solution should help teams:

- reduce repetitive manual work
- improve consistency of task execution
- support faster navigation through complex workflows
- surface useful recommendations without over-automating
- make AI outputs easier to review and trust

## Example Use-Case Areas

Examples of relevant use-case areas include:

- risk and compliance support
- workflow design and documentation
- IT support and routing
- onboarding and service delivery
- audit and operations task management
- research and analysis workflows
- cross-platform knowledge and assistance

## Operating Principle

The goal is not to automate everything. The goal is to use AI agents where they can improve workflow outcomes, while keeping appropriate human judgment, review, and control in place.
